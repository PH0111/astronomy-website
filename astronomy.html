  • Vacuum

    Space is primarily characterized by an extremely low density of matter, making it a near-perfect vacuum. There are very few particles, such as atoms and molecules, in outer space compared to Earth's atmosphere. This vacuum is one of the reasons why humans need specialized spacesuits and spacecraft to survive and operate in space.

  • Dark Energy

    Dark energy is a mysterious force that is causing the expansion of the universe to accelerate. Its nature is not yet fully understood, but it appears to make up a significant portion of the universe's energy content.

  • Cosmic radiation

    It is a stream of high-energy particles (such as protons and electrons), gamma rays and high-energy X-rays that come from outer space and head toward Earth.

  • dark matter

    It is a concept in astronomy and astrophysics that refers to a mysterious invisible and non-radioactive substance, Based on astronomical evidence, dark matter is thought to make up a very large part of matter in the universe. It is estimated that dark matter accounts for about 27% of the total content of the universe.

  • Nebula

    It is a collection of dust and hot gases in space. The nebula is often seen as a dark cloud or blurry range in space and appears distinctively from the stellar background.
